Conclusion, after checking every distinct branch in the tree (not just one path): your verified Arecibo ancestry runs cleanly through María Ramona Román and through Amado Marrero Montalvo's Marrero/Montalvo grandparents — all real, sourced Puerto Rico records, ending where FamilySearch has no parents recorded. The Colombian Jurado/Sánchez/Herrera/Aguirre/Agudelo family, wrongly attached at María Ramona Román, is itself genuinely well-documented all the way back to 1554 in Spain — it's a real family, just not yours. The chain only turns fictional at two specific points: María Yamecí Indígena (zero sources, grafted onto an otherwise real 1510s Spanish line) and the "royal descent" branch through Moctezuma I and medieval Spanish nobility. Fixing this on FamilySearch means detaching Anatilde Román Jurado from María Ramona Román's profile — everything past that single edit is the unrelated Colombian family's own tree, not yours.
